## 0. Premises (inherited from jiwa.md, normative)

Three premises precede every brand decision:

1. **Manusia adalah makhluk kolektif.** Digital platforms manufacture isolation (algorithmic bubbles, mirror feeds); alternatif.space begins as a refusal of that isolation. The first thing the brand must make a visitor feel is not a feature — it is *kamu bagian dari sesuatu*. Kolektif bukan berarti sepakat; kolektif berarti **hadir bersama**.
2. **"Partai" artinya kelompok.** KBBI: *par·tai /n/ perkumpulan (segolongan orang) yang seasas, sehaluan, dan setujuan.* The platform reclaims the word from election machines and patronage. This is an argument, not a rebranding — and the brand should make the argument openly in copy. The KBBI entry itself is a first-class copy asset.
3. **Perbedaan adalah fitur, bukan bug.** Difference is raw material, not a risk to be managed. The brand promise is structured tension: *wadah yang cukup kuat untuk menampung kekacauan yang bermakna*. The brand must never sell harmony, safety-from-disagreement, or consensus-as-product.

## 1. The Soul, in One Paragraph

Indonesia sudah punya kata untuk demokrasi yang baik — **musyawarah untuk mufakat**. Kata itu ada di sila keempat Pancasila, dihafal setiap anak sekolah, dan hampir tidak pernah dipraktikkan oleh sistem politik formal. alternatif.space bukan mengimpor demokrasi dari luar; ia **mengembalikan praktik yang sudah jadi milik kita** — dengan infrastruktur digital yang membuat praktik itu mungkin dilakukan lintas pulau, lintas kelas, lintas posisi. The platform is civic education disguised as political infrastructure. Its soul is the gap between a word every Indonesian knows and an experience almost no Indonesian has had.

## 2. Brand Essence

**Ruang latihan demokrasi.** (A practice space for democracy.)

Not a party directory, not political social media, not an opposition movement. A *dojo*: a structured space where the practice itself — forming positions, deliberating in good faith, building consensus, governing collectively — is the product.

### 2.1 The Emotional Promise (dual, ordered)

Jiwa.md establishes that the platform's most fundamental purpose is the feeling of being part of something. The brand promise therefore has two layers, in order:

1. **Kamu tidak sendirian.** (Against political loneliness — the precondition. Premis 1.)
2. **Di sini posisimu dianggap serius.** (Even when people disagree with it; especially then. The differentiator.)

Landing and onboarding copy should deliver 1 before 2: belonging first, rigor second. Rigor without belonging reads as a debate club; belonging without rigor reads as another social feed.

## 3. The Audience Truth

The target user is under 40, digitally native, and politically *hungry but homeless*:

- They have only ever experienced politics as spectacle — "gemoy" campaigns, buzzer wars, personality cults. They are fluent in irony about it.
- They distrust institutions but crave agency. "Politik itu kotor" is what they say; "tidak ada tempat untuk saya" is what they mean. Jiwa names the deeper layer: they feel *politically alone* — they have views but no room to bring them before other views.
- They will smell propaganda, partisanship, and government-circular language instantly. They reward honesty, transparency, and being treated as adults.
- They speak casual Indonesian (kamu, nggak, yuk) but respect seriousness of substance. Gaya boleh santai; isi tidak boleh kosong.
- Many recoil from the word "partai" itself — not from apathy but from repeated disappointment. The brand meets this head-on with the KBBI reclamation (§0.2), never by avoiding the word.

## 4. Brand Pillars

1. **Posisi, bukan persona.** Ideas over personalities. Membership is a flag (bendera), arguments carry the flag, debate shifts from *who you are* to *where you stand*. This is the platform's sharpest differentiator and its founding thesis. (Jiwa: the bendera on every comment is not aesthetics — it is a continuous reminder that a position is speaking, not a persona.)
2. **Praktik, bukan teori.** Democracy is learned by doing. Every mechanic (manifesto wajib, recall, konfigurasi terbuka) is a rep in the gym, not a feature.
3. **Mufakat sebagai warisan.** Deliberation-to-consensus is an Indonesian inheritance being reclaimed, not a Western import being localized. The subdomain names (partai, mufakat, simposium, perpus) already carry this — the brand should own it.
4. **Jujur sampai tulang.** Open source, transparent finances, "unresolved is a valid state," "this might fail." Honesty is not a compliance posture; it is the aesthetic. (Jiwa extends this: *"kita belum tahu"* is a legitimate, valuable conclusion — the platform is a mesin pemikiran bersama, not a mesin konsensus.)

### 4.1 The Anatomy (one idea, four moments)

The four subdomains are not four products; they are one idea split across four moments of the same social dynamic — **anatomi dari ketidaksetujuan yang sehat**:

| Moment | Subdomain | What happens |
|---|---|---|
| **Identitas** | partai | Positions form; you wear a bendera; you speak as a position, not an anonymous individual |
| **Pertemuan** | mufakat | Flags meet across party lines; difference becomes conversation, not distance |
| **Deliberasi** | simposium | Friction is channeled; arguments become understanding — a clearer map of where the difference actually lies |
| **Dokumentasi** | perpus | Collective memory; what was settled, what stays open — and "open" is a valid state |

Identitas → pertemuan → deliberasi → dokumentasi: a repeatable cycle, each repetition building deeper capacity. All narrative copy (landing W0-02/W0-03, onboarding, press) should present the subdomains as this cycle, never as a feature list.

## 5. Voice

| Dimension | Decision |
|---|---|
| **Register** | Santai-serius: casual grammar (nggak, yuk allowed), serious vocabulary. Never gaul (no lo/gue, no trend words — sat-set, no debat, bestie are banned). Never baku resmi (no "tersebut", no "melakukan pendaftaran"). |
| **Pronoun** | `kamu` everywhere public-facing. `Anda` never. Brand first-person: `kita` when including the reader in the project ("aturan yang kita tulis sendiri"), `kami` only for operational/accountability statements ("kode kami terbuka"). |
| **Persona** | *Teman diskusi yang tajam* — a sharp peer at the warung kopi table who has done the reading but never lectures. Asks real questions. Admits what's unresolved. |
| **Banned modes** | Government circular (baku mati), buzzer hype (caps, emoji walls, fake urgency), NGO grant-speak ("pemberdayaan", "sinergi"), campaign promises ("untuk Indonesia yang lebih baik"). |

Jiwa.md itself is the reference text for this voice: declarative, unhedged, warm without hype, willing to say "platform ini mungkin gagal." When in doubt, write the way jiwa.md is written.

### Lexicon

**Kata kita** (use): posisi, bendera, manifesto, mufakat, musyawarah, deliberasi, suara, aturan main, ruang, latihan, terbuka, recall, dirikan, **kolektif, hadir bersama, gesekan, seasas, sehaluan**.
**Kata haram** (avoid): sinergi, kolaborasi (as filler), revolusi, perubahan (unqualified), viral, gabung yuk! (as hype), demokrasi digital (as buzzword), platform (in user-facing copy — say *ruang*).
**Copy asset:** the KBBI entry — *par·tai /n/ perkumpulan (segolongan orang) yang seasas, sehaluan, dan setujuan* — may be quoted verbatim, dictionary-style, as a rhetorical device (landing, onboarding, share cards).

### Sound

Indonesian copy here leans on **antitesis** (posisi/persona, suara/wajah, isi/kemasan, cermin/jendela) and **triplet rhythm** (dirikan, tulis, bawa). Hyperbole is off-brand; understatement and precision are on-brand. The platform's honesty extends to its grammar.

## 6. Cultural Anchors

- **Sila keempat, Pancasila** — "…kerakyatan yang dipimpin oleh hikmat kebijaksanaan dalam permusyawaratan/perwakilan." The platform is arguably the most literal implementation of sila-4 on the Indonesian internet. Use the *concept* (musyawarah, mufakat, hikmat) — never state flags, never garuda imagery, never anything that reads as state cosplay or nationalism-as-decoration.
- **KBBI as authority** — quoting the dictionary to reclaim "partai" borrows the most neutral institutional authority Indonesia has. It is anti-elite without being anti-institution: *the word was always ours.*
- **Bendera** — the membership flag mechanic maps to a deep Indonesian instinct: kampung flags, organisasi colors, almamater jackets. Identity worn openly so the argument can be about substance.
- **Warung discourse** — Indonesia's real deliberative space has always been informal: warung kopi, pos ronda, angkringan. The voice belongs there, not in a seminar room.
- **What to refuse:** partisan color codes (avoid reading as red/green/blue party), Western civic-tech aesthetic clichés (flat illustration people holding hands), and any visual that implies the platform itself has a political position. The platform is the *table*, not a *seat* at it.

## 7. Naming Logic (existing, made explicit)

Every subdomain is a plain Indonesian institutional noun: **partai** (form), **mufakat** (deliberate — from *musyawarah untuk mufakat*), **simposium** (convene), **perpus** (remember). The root domain completes the sentence: **alternatif** — not "the opposition," but *the alternative way of doing politics*. Copy should occasionally let the domain name do semantic work: "Cari alternatif? Bangun sendiri."

## 8. Design Implications (input to landing options)

- Mobile-first, fast on mid-range Android over 4G — performance is brand honesty.
- Typography carries the brand (no mascots, no stock illustration). Editorial confidence over decoration.
- The existing partai app palette (ink `#1A1714`, amber `#F5A623`, paper `#FEF3DC`; Instrument Serif + DM Sans) is one legitimate expression, not a constraint — but whichever landing direction wins should be reconciled with the app identity in a follow-up token pass.
- Amber/gold is a strong neutral-but-warm civic color for Indonesia: lampu warung, padi, brass gamelan — warm, common-ground, non-partisan.
- Dark/ink surfaces signal seriousness; warm paper surfaces signal heritage; both are honest. Neon/hype gradients are not.

## 9. Brand Guardrails (from jiwa.md "Apa yang Harus Dilindungi")

These are failure modes the brand must never drift into, restated as copy/design rules:

- **Jangan bersihkan gesekannya.** Never market the platform as "safe", "moderated", or "ruang aman dari konflik". The product sells productive tension, not tranquility. The mufakat split mechanic is on-brand; copy that apologizes for disagreement is not.
- **Jangan biarkan partai jadi gema.** Cross-party encounter is existential, not optional. Copy must never frame a partai as a destination community ("temukan komunitasmu dan nyamanlah di sana"); it is a base camp for going *out* to mufakat.
- **Jangan takut pada kesimpulan terbuka.** "Belum selesai" and "ini lebih rumit dari yang kita kira" are legitimate endings in any copy surface (perpus especially). Never manufacture closure.
- **Jangan biarkan kepribadian mengalahkan posisi.** No founder-facing brand, no testimonial-of-the-famous, no avatar prominence over bendera prominence — in marketing as in product.

## 10. What Success Sounds Like (from jiwa.md, for all narrative copy)

Not user counts, party counts, or virality. The stories the brand tells (press, about page, year-in-review) are:

- Someone entered with a view, spent time arguing with people who disagreed, and left understanding more — either changed, or knowing *precisely where* and *why* they differ.
- A digital party that began as an experiment became, years later, a real movement.
- A young Indonesian experienced thinking together with people who differ — for the first time — and found it not frightening but alive.

And the brand keeps jiwa's honesty about stakes: *ini bukan produk; ini eksperimen demokrasi.* That sentence is quotable, and should be.
